Introduction to IOT

Internet of Things is a system of devices connected to the Internet with the ability to collect and exchange data from users or environment with no human intervention.

The device or the 'Things' in IOT could be any device embedded with electronics, software and sensor like a smart refrigerator, a smart air conditioner,lights in household,connected security system or even a person with a heart monitor or an automobile.

Why IOT?

    we are lazy.

    we want to automate everything.

    we want to control everything remotely.

    we want to see data in real time.

Little History

    IOT was originated in the late 1990's when the concept of connecting everyday object to the internet was introduced.

    The term "Internet of Things" was conined by Kelvin Ashton in 1999.

    Early IOT devices, like a toaster that could be controlled remotely, showcasted the potential of IOT.

Applications of IOT

  • Building and Home Automation

  • Smart Manufacturing

  • Medical and healthcare systems

  • Smart Transportation

  • Energy resource management

  • Ability to access information from anywhere at any time on any device;

  • Improved communication between connected electronics devices;

  • Transferring data packets over a connected network saving time and money;

  • Automating tasks helping to improve the quality of a business's services and reducing the need for human intervention.

  • As the number of connected devices increases and more information is shared between devices,the potential that a hacker could steal confidential information increases.

  • If there's a bug in the system, it's likely that every connected device will become corrupted.

  • Enterprises may eventually have to deal with massive numbers--maybe even millions--of IOT devices, and collecting and managing the data from all those devices will be challenging.

  • Since there's no international standard of compatability for IOT, it's difficult for devices from manufactures to commtnicate with each others.

    • Examples of IOT

  • Imagine a smart traffic camera:The camera can monitor the road for congestion,accident and weather conditions and communicate that's data to a gateway that combines it with data from other such cameras creating an intelligent citywide traffic system.

  • A lightbulb that can be switched on using a smartphone app is an IOT device, as is a motion sensor or a smart thermostant in your office or a connected streetlight.

  • Some large object may themselves be filled with many smaller IOT components, stch as a jet engine that's now filled with thousands of sensors collecting and transmitrting data back to make sure it operating effciently.

    • Challanges of IOT

  • Sensing a complex environment:Innovative ways to sense and deliver information from physical world to the cloud.

  • Connectivity:Variety of wired and wireless standards are required to enable different application needs.

  • Power:Many IOT applications need to run for years over battery and reduce the overall energy consumption.

  • Security:Protecting user's privacy, manufacturer's Ip and blocking malicious activity.

  • Compliexity:IOT application development needs to be easy for all developers not just to experts.

  • Cloud:IOT application require end-to-end solutions including cloud services.
